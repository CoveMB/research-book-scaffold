# Claims and argument

Use `30-claims-and-argument/` to test what the manuscript may argue.

This layer comes after evidence and analysis. It is where possible claims get an
evidence status, and where claims are arranged into an argument that can survive
objections.

Subfolders:

- `claim-ledger/` for proposed claims, support, counterevidence, confidence, and
  follow-up tasks.
- `argument-maps/` for warrants, dependencies, objections, counterarguments, and
  weak links between claims.

Use this layer before chapter planning. A claim can move toward drafting only
when its source trail, citekeys, locators, and caveats are visible.

Do not upgrade a claim because it sounds plausible. If support is thin, record a
safer wording or a research task.
