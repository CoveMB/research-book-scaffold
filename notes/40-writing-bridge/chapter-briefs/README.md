# Chapter briefs

Use `chapter-briefs/` to plan chapters and sections before drafting.

Create a brief when a chapter or section has enough evidence, claims, and
argument structure to plan responsibly. A brief should make the writing task
clear without hiding gaps.

Include:

- Chapter or section purpose.
- Central question and provisional thesis.
- Argument sequence.
- Evidence placement and likely sources.
- Counterarguments and definitions to track.
- Gaps that must remain visible in the draft.

Do not draft directly from a brief unless the linked notes, claim records, and
citekeys support the claims being moved into prose.
