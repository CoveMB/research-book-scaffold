# Analysis

Use `20-analysis/` for cross-source thinking.

This layer comes after source notes, case notes, extraction tables, or source
matrices have enough material to compare. It is where you map debates, define
terms, and write cautious synthesis before turning anything into a claim.

Subfolders:

- `literature-maps/` for debates, schools of thought, source clusters, gaps, and
  relationships among sources.
- `concept-notes/` for definitions, boundaries, competing usages, and risks of
  misuse.
- `synthesis-memos/` for provisional cross-source interpretation, uncertainty,
  counterevidence, and next steps.

Use this layer when the question is larger than one source but not yet ready for
draft prose. When analysis supports a specific manuscript claim, move that claim
into `30-claims-and-argument/claim-ledger/` with evidence status and locators.

Do not turn provisional synthesis into final proof. Keep disagreement and weak
support visible.
